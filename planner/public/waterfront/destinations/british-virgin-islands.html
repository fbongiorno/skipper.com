The Sailing Capital of the World

Where Trade Winds
Meet Turquoise Seas

The British Virgin Islands are widely considered the finest sailing destination on the planet. Steady northeast trade winds, protected channels between closely spaced islands, crystal-clear anchorages, and a relaxed maritime culture make the BVI an experience unlike anywhere else on earth.

With over 60 islands, cays, and rocks strung across just 50 nautical miles, you're never more than a short sail from the next hidden bay. The passages are benign, the weather predictable, and the welcome warm. Whether you're an experienced bluewater sailor or stepping aboard for the first time, the BVI rewards.

Top Destinations

Six Unmissable Stops

01
Virgin Gorda

The Baths

Enormous granite boulders forming cathedral-like sea grottoes and tidal pools. The most iconic anchorage in the entire Caribbean — arrive before 9am to beat the day-charter crowds.

National Park mooring balls available · Dinghy ashore · 0.5mi trail to Devils Bay
02
Jost Van Dyke

White Bay

Home of the legendary Soggy Dollar Bar and the original Painkiller cocktail. A curving white-sand beach with a laid-back atmosphere and local population of just 300.

Anchor or mooring ball · Customs at Great Harbour · Swim ashore
03
Norman Island

The Bight

Said to have inspired Robert Louis Stevenson's Treasure Island. The Bight offers the BVI's best-protected anchorage. William T's floating bar is a beloved institution.

Well-protected · Sea Caves 0.5nm NE · Excellent snorkeling at The Indians
04
Tortola

Cane Garden Bay

Tortola's most celebrated beach. A wide crescent of golden sand lined with beach bars and Quito's — the island's beloved live music venue. Best visited in calm weather.

Shallow approach — check draft · Anchor in 10–20ft · Avoid in N swells
05
Anegada

Anegada

The BVI's only coral island — flat, remote and utterly wild. Famous for fresh-caught lobster, a flamingo colony, pristine beaches stretching for miles, and precise navigation requirements.

GPS essential · Reef approach — follow waypoints exactly · 14nm N of Tortola
06
Cooper Island

Cooper Island

A low-key eco resort with an excellent beach bar, rum bar, and dive shop. Superb coral reef snorkeling directly off the mooring field. Arrive early — mooring balls fill by noon.

Mooring balls only · Reserve ahead · Excellent reef off bow

7-Day Circuit

Starting and ending from Road Town or Nanny Cay marina, Tortola. Covers the best of the main island group plus an optional run to Anegada. Suitable for moderate sailing experience.

Essential Knowledge

The Sailor's BVI Guide

Everything you need to know before you cast off the dock lines in Road Town.

Navigation & Charts

The BVI uses NOAA chart 25641 as the primary reference. All anchorages are charted, but use GPS and visual confirmation — especially approaching Anegada's reef. Most cruising apps (Navionics, iNavX, Aqua Map) carry detailed BVI coverage.

  • Monitor VHF Channel 16 at all times
  • Working channels: 68, 69, 71, 78A
  • Sir Francis Drake Channel is the main highway
  • No anchoring on coral — use National Park Trust mooring balls
  • Mooring ball fee: ~$25–30 USD per night (pay the ranger)

Customs & Clearance

All foreign-flagged vessels must clear customs on arrival. The BVI uses the CBSA online system (eSeaClear) — complete your crew list and vessel details before entering BVI waters.

  • Clearance ports: Road Town (Tortola), Great Harbour (JVD), Spanish Town (VG)
  • US Virgin Islands → BVI: clear in at first BVI port of call
  • Cruising permit required for stays over 24h
  • Keep ship's papers, passports and proof of ownership accessible
  • Clearance hours: 8:30am–4:30pm (Mon–Fri); overtime fees apply weekends

Weather & Season

The BVI sits firmly in the trade wind belt. From December through June expect reliable ENE winds of 15–25 knots — ideal beam-reaching between islands. Summers bring lighter, more variable winds.

  • Peak season: December–April (settled, consistent trades)
  • Shoulder season: May–June & November (fewer boats, good winds)
  • Hurricane season: June–November (avoid August–October)
  • Water temp: 26–29°C year round
  • VHF weather: NOAA WX1–WX3 and Caribbean Weather Centre

Provisioning & Marinas

Road Town on Tortola is the charter hub and best provisioning stop. Nanny Cay Marina and Village Cay Marina both offer full services, fuel docks, and chandleries. Replenish before heading to outer islands.

  • Nanny Cay: full haul-out, fuel, chandlery, restaurant, Wi-Fi
  • Village Cay: central Road Town location, dockage & provisions
  • Leverick Bay (Virgin Gorda): fuel, provisions, good restaurant
  • Anegada Settlement: limited provisions — stock up on Tortola
  • Diesel: ~$4.50–5.50/gallon USD at most fuel docks

At a Glance

Practical Information

TerritoryBritish Virgin Islands — UK Overseas Territory. No BVI citizenship required for visitors. Warm, English-speaking welcome.
CurrencyUS Dollar (USD). ATMs available in Road Town. Most marinas and restaurants accept major credit cards. Carry cash for mooring ball fees and beach bars.
LanguageEnglish. No language barrier for English-speaking sailors.
Charter BasesRoad Town (Nanny Cay, Village Cay, Wickhams Cay II), Hodges Creek Marina. All major charter companies operate here: Moorings, Sunsail, Dream Yacht, Footloose.
Documents RequiredValid passport for all crew. Ship's registration papers. Proof of insurance. Crew list (minimum 2 copies for customs). Certificate of competency (bareboat charters may require RYA or USCG qualification).
Emergency ContactsBVI Coast Guard: VHF Ch 16 or +1 (284) 494-3999. VISAR (Virgin Islands Search and Rescue): +1 (284) 499-0911 or VHF Ch 16. US Coast Guard San Juan: Ch 16.
Time ZoneAtlantic Standard Time (AST) — UTC-4. No daylight saving time observed.
Best Charter MonthsDecember–April for perfect trade winds and settled weather. November and May–June offer the same winds with fewer boats and lower prices. Avoid August–October.
Dress CodeCasual throughout. Swimwear is standard attire at beach bars. Cover up when entering shops or government buildings in Road Town.
Marine EnvironmentBVI is a protected marine sanctuary. No fishing in National Park areas. No taking of coral, shells or marine life. Use BVI-approved biodegradable sunscreen (reef-safe) — standard chemical sunscreen is banned.
