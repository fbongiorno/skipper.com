The Ocean Sailor's Paradise

Where the Indian Ocean
Becomes Infinite

The Maldives is unlike any sailing destination on earth. 1,200 coral islands spread across 26 atolls in the middle of the Indian Ocean, ringed by the world's finest coral reefs. The water is a translucent turquoise of impossible clarity, and beneath the surface lies a world of whale sharks, manta rays, hammerhead sharks, and endless coral gardens.

Sailing here is liveaboard territory — distances between atolls require overnight passages, and many of the finest anchorages are accessible only to those who arrive by boat. Charter from Malé and discover islands with no roads, no electricity, and no footprints.

The surreal geometry of a Maldivian atoll viewed from the water — coral reef, lagoon, and open ocean in perfect juxtaposition

"In the Maldives, the sea is not a backdrop — it is everything. The water, the reef, the sky, and the silence become the entire world."

Top Destinations

Six Unmissable Atolls

01
UNESCO Biosphere

Baa Atoll

A UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and home of Hanifaru Bay — where dozens of manta rays and whale sharks congregate to feed in the nutrient-rich waters from June to November. The most spectacular marine wildlife experience in the world.

Permit required for Hanifaru Bay · Limited boats allowed · Best Aug–Oct for aggregations
02
Whale Shark Capital

Ari Atoll

South Ari Atoll is the world's most reliable spot for year-round whale shark encounters. These gentle giants feed in the lagoon daily. Snorkel or dive alongside the ocean's largest fish in water of extraordinary clarity.

Year-round whale sharks · South Ari Marine Protected Area · Best depth: 5–20m
03
Charter Hub

North Malé Atoll

The atoll closest to the capital and the starting point for most charters. Ringed by world-class surf breaks and dive sites. The uninhabited island of Baros offers stunning snorkeling directly off a pristine beach.

Closest atoll to Malé · Multiple surf breaks · 30min from international airport
04
Hammerhead Reef

Rasdhoo Atoll

A small, jewel-like atoll famous for Rasdhoo Madivaru — one of the world's best dive sites for hammerhead sharks. Best dived at dawn. The local island of Rasdhoo town is a genuine, welcoming Maldivian community.

Hammerhead sharks at dawn · Drift dives on outer wall · Local island welcome
05
Remote Paradise

Fulhadhoo Island

One of the most remote and beautiful uninhabited islands accessible by charter. A flawless white sand beach stretches for kilometres, fringed by crystal-clear water and a pristine house reef with no other boat in sight.

No resort, no people · House reef for snorkeling · Anchor in protected lagoon
06
Diving & Reefs

Lhaviyani Atoll

The northern atoll celebrated for its pristine coral gardens, exceptional visibility, and the Kuredu Express — a fast-current dive channel teeming with sharks, rays and thousands of fish. One of the Maldives' top dive destinations.

World-class visibility (40m+) · Kuredu Express channel dive · Overnight passage from Malé

Essential Knowledge

The Sailor's Maldives Guide

Everything you need to know before you cast off the dock lines in Malé and head into the Indian Ocean.

Navigation & Reef Awareness

Maldivian waters are among the most navigationally challenging on earth. Shallow reef patches, atoll passes with strong currents, and thousands of unmarked coral heads require constant GPS vigilance. Never navigate without current, up-to-date charts.

  • Use Navionics or C-Map with frequent updates
  • Atoll passes best transited at slack water
  • Never enter unfamiliar lagoons without aerial or drone recon first
  • Strong currents in inter-atoll channels — 3–5 knots common
  • Anchor on sand only — coral damage carries severe fines
  • VHF Ch 16 monitored by Maldives Coast Guard

Permits & Regulations

The Maldives has strict regulations for foreign vessels. A Yacht Cruising Permit is mandatory before arrival in Maldivian waters. Apply through the Maldives Ports Authority well in advance.

  • Yacht Cruising Permit: apply at maldivesports.gov.mv
  • Entry port: Malé or Hulhumalé marina
  • Clearance: Customs, Immigration, Health and Port Authority
  • No anchoring within resort island boundaries (200m exclusion zone)
  • Fishing with commercial gear prohibited
  • Environmental protection regulations strictly enforced

Monsoons & Wind

The Maldives has two distinct monsoons. The northeast monsoon (November–April) brings the finest sailing conditions — light, steady winds of 4–10 knots and excellent visibility. The southwest monsoon (May–October) brings rain, stronger winds and rougher seas.

  • Best season: December–April (NE monsoon, settled seas)
  • Shoulder season: November and May (transitional, variable)
  • SW monsoon: May–October (rain, stronger winds 15–25kn)
  • Cyclones rare but possible June–November
  • Water visibility: 20–40m during NE monsoon
  • Sea temperature: 28–30°C year round

Provisioning & Charter Types

Malé is the only significant provisioning stop in the Maldives. Outer atolls have limited or no supplies. Plan every meal carefully before leaving the capital. Most charters in the Maldives operate as liveaboards — sleeping aboard and moving daily.

  • Malé harbour: best provisioning stop, full supplies
  • Hulhumalé marina: modern facilities, fuel dock
  • Liveaboard charter (most common): expert crew navigate for you
  • Bareboat charter: requires advanced offshore certification
  • Provisions for remote atolls: full 7-day stock from Malé
  • Freshwater: tanks only — no shore fill beyond Malé

At a Glance

Practical Information

CountryRepublic of Maldives. Independent nation. English widely spoken throughout the tourism sector.
CurrencyMaldivian Rufiyaa (MVR), but US Dollars (USD) universally accepted at marinas, resorts and charter companies.
LanguageDhivehi (official). English fluently spoken in all marine and hospitality contexts.
Charter BasesMalé and Hulhumalé marina. Liveaboard operators: Sea Spirit, Maldives Aggressor, Emperor Fleet. Bareboat charter: limited — requires offshore certification.
Best SeasonDecember–April (NE monsoon). Peak: January–March for settled conditions, whale shark encounters in South Ari, and extraordinary underwater visibility.
Emergency ContactsMaldives Coast Guard: VHF Ch 16 or +960 331-9999. MNDF Coast Guard: +960 332-0918. Emergency services: 119.
Time ZoneMaldives Standard Time (MVT) — UTC+5. No daylight saving time.
Marine EnvironmentOne of the world's most protected marine environments. Zero tolerance for coral damage, fishing in MPAs, or anchoring on reef. Sunscreen: reef-safe only.
VisaMost nationalities receive 30-day visa on arrival at no charge. Yacht Cruising Permit must be obtained separately before entry.
