The Cradle of Sailing Civilisation

Ancient Seas,
Eternal Light

The Cyclades islands are where sailing was born. For four millennia, mariners have navigated these cobalt-blue waters, driven by the same trade winds that now fill the sails of today's yachts. The Aegean rewards with a sensory richness found nowhere else — dazzling white-washed villages against volcanic rock, olive groves scenting the air, and harbours where ancient marble columns stand at the waterline.

Santorini's caldera is one of the world's great sailing experiences: a flooded volcanic crater 12km wide, with cliff walls rising 300 metres above the water. Sail in through the narrow gap, anchor in 30 metres of crystal-clear water, and look up at the spectacle of Fira and Oia clinging impossibly to the rim.

The iconic whitewashed domes of Oia perched on Santorini's caldera rim — best viewed from the water at sunset

"Santorini's caldera is the most dramatic anchorage in the Mediterranean. You sail in through an ancient volcanic gap and anchor in 30 metres of history, with white villages shining 1,000 feet above your mast."

Top Destinations

Six Unmissable Stops

01
Volcanic Marvel

Santorini Caldera

Sail through the narrow Akrotiri passage into the drowned caldera of a supervolcano. Anchor in the dramatic 300m-deep crater with sheer black cliffs on all sides and the white villages of Fira and Oia shining at the rim. Incomparable at sunset.

Deep anchorage 30–50m · Stern-to mooring in Ammoudi Bay · Visit Nea Kameni hot springs by dinghy
02
Iconic Sunset

Oia & Ammoudi Bay

The most celebrated sunset in the world. Anchor in Ammoudi Bay beneath the clifftop village, climb the 300 steps to Oia, and watch the sun descend into the caldera as the sky turns every shade of gold and crimson. Photographs cannot prepare you.

Ammoudi Bay: limited mooring space · Arrive by 2pm in summer · Steps to Oia: 300 steep steps
03
Sea Caves

Milos — Kleftiko

Milos is the Cyclades' hidden gem. The southwest coastline features Kleftiko — spectacular white volcanic rock formations, sea caves, and arches accessible only by boat. Former pirate lair, now the finest natural anchorage in the Aegean.

Access by boat only · Multiple caves for swimming · Crystal clear water
04
Island Life

Mykonos

Cosmopolitan, vibrant and perpetually beautiful. Little Venice at the waterfront, windmills on the hill, and white-washed labyrinthine streets. Anchor in Ornos Bay for a quieter option, or brave the new marina for the full Mykonos experience.

New Mykonos Marina: full services · Ornos Bay: quieter anchorage · Meltemi strong in July–Aug
05
Largest Cyclades Island

Naxos

The Cyclades' largest and most fertile island. Excellent provisioning in Naxos Town (Hora), a magnificent ancient portara gateway standing alone on the waterfront, and the beautiful Agia Anna beach just south of town. Well-protected harbour.

Best provisioning in the Cyclades · Agia Anna beach 2nm south · Portara gateway at waterfront
06
Windsurfer's Island

Paros

The gold standard for Cyclades sailing — consistent Meltemi winds, beautiful harbours at Parikia and Naoussa, excellent tavernas, and a relaxed atmosphere. The Bay of Naoussa is one of the most picturesque anchorages in the Aegean.

Two excellent harbours · Naoussa: anchorage or marina · Strong afternoon Meltemi — reliable sailing
Sample Route

The Classic Cyclades
7-Day Circuit

Starting from Piraeus/Athens (Alimos Marina or Zea Marina). Covers the finest highlights of the Cyclades including the unmissable Santorini caldera. Suitable for moderately experienced sailors — Meltemi awareness essential.

Depart Alimos or Zea Marina heading SE. Reach beautiful Hydra with its car-free colourful port. Evening taverna in the harbour.

Kleftiko at Sunset
Long passage south. Arrive at the spectacular Kleftiko sea caves on Milos's SW coast for sunset anchoring among white volcanic formations.
~45nm · 8h

Enter the Caldera
Sail east. Enter Santorini caldera through the narrow Akrotiri gap. Anchor in 30–50m of clear volcanic water beneath sheer 300m cliffs.
~35nm · 6h

Fira, Oia & Hot Springs
Explore Fira and Oia by day. Visit Nea Kameni hot springs by dinghy. Sunset from Ammoudi Bay steps — the finest in the Mediterranean.

5
Santorini → Naxos
Ios Stop & Provision
Quick stop at Ios beach then north to Naxos Town. Best provisioning in the Cyclades — stock up the galley. Portara gateway at the waterfront.

Naxos → Paros
Naoussa Anchorage

Long but rewarding return passage north. Consider an overnight passage if preferred to avoid the full day sail. Depart early to make the most of the Meltemi.

Essential Knowledge

The Sailor's Cyclades Guide

Everything you need to know before you cast off from Piraeus and point south into the Aegean.

The Meltemi Wind

The Meltemi is the dominant feature of Aegean sailing — a strong, dry NW–N wind that blows reliably from June to September. It can reach Force 6–7 (25–33 knots) in July and August. Once you understand it, the Meltemi is your ally; it is predictable, daytime-dominated, and excellent for fast island-hopping.

  • Meltemi strongest: July–August (can reach F7 with short notice)
  • Meltemi typically eases by late evening
  • Early morning passages recommended in peak summer
  • Always check Poseidon weather forecast (poseidon.hcmr.gr) before departing
  • Wind direction: NW to N in northern Cyclades, N to NE around Santorini
  • Windward anchorages (south/east facing) essential in strong Meltemi

Navigation & Ferry Traffic

The Aegean is one of the world's busiest ferry networks. High-speed hydrofoils and overnight ferries operate at speed through the same channels as sailing yachts. Maintain sharp watch at all times, particularly at night.

  • Monitor VHF Ch 16 and Ch 12 (Piraeus Port)
  • Major ferry routes bisect the Cyclades — maintain clear of fairways
  • Santorini caldera: 30–50m depth — use modern anchor chain of sufficient length
  • Day marks and lights on most islands well maintained
  • Greek Hydrographic charts (HNHS) for detailed island coverage
  • Night passages possible but ferry awareness essential

Customs & Greece

Greece is a full EU and Schengen member. EU-flagged vessels circulate freely. Non-EU vessels must check in at a Port of Entry on arrival.

  • Non-EU vessels: fly Q flag until customs clearance
  • Port of Entry options: Piraeus, Rhodes, Corfu, Kos, Heraklion, Kavala
  • EU vessel safety equipment: Greek law may require additional flares/life raft
  • Hellenic Coast Guard regular patrols — paperwork must be current
  • Transit log (biblio dimou): required for non-EU vessels
  • Crew changes: must be logged with port authorities

Provisioning in the Cyclades

Naxos offers the best provisioning in the Cyclades — large supermarkets, chandlery, fuel dock and good marina. Athens/Piraeus is your best stock-up point before departure. Outer islands have limited supplies and high prices.

  • Alimos Marina (Athens): best full-service chandlery base
  • Naxos Town: best island provisioning, full supermarket
  • Mykonos: available but expensive — tourist prices apply
  • Fuel docks: Piraeus, Naxos, Mykonos, Santorini (Vlychada)
  • Ice: marinas only — carry a good cooler
  • Fresh produce: excellent quality at island morning markets

At a Glance

Practical Information

Country Greece, European Union. Schengen Area member. English widely spoken in all maritime and tourist contexts.
Currency Euro (€). ATMs available on all inhabited islands. Credit cards accepted at most marinas and restaurants.
Language Greek (official). English fluently spoken throughout the yachting and tourism sector.
Charter Bases Alimos Marina (Athens) and Zea Marina (Piraeus) are the main departure points. Operators: Sunsail, Moorings, Navigare Yachting, Vernicos Yachts.
Best Season May–June and September–October for the best balance of warm weather, manageable Meltemi, and fewer charter boats. July–August is peak season — very busy.
Emergency Contacts Hellenic Coast Guard: VHF Ch 16 or +30 210-41-99-00. Athens Rescue Coordination Centre: +30 210-519-2606. Emergency services: 112.
Time Zone Eastern European Time (EET) — UTC+2. Summer (EEST): UTC+3.
Marine Environment Mediterranean Protected Areas. Strict Posidonia seagrass protection (heavy fines for anchoring on meadows). Use mooring buoys in protected bays where available.
Cultural Note Greek hospitality is legendary but dress codes apply ashore — cover up when visiting churches and monasteries. Remove shoes in many island chapels.
